Phishing Scams

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations — banks, government agencies, or social media platforms — to steal sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, and credit card numbers through deceptive emails, SMS, or fake websites.

Red Flags
  • Urgent messages demanding immediate action
  • Sender email doesn't match the official domain
  • Suspicious links (hover to see the real URL)
  • Requests for OTP, password, or card details
  • Poor grammar and spelling in messages
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers or lottery wins
How to Stay Safe
  • Never share OTP, PIN, or passwords — even with "bank officials"
  • Verify the sender email carefully before clicking
  • Access websites by typing the URL directly
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Use a password manager for strong, unique passwords
  • Report phishing emails to report@phishing.gov.in

"Urgent: Your SBI account has been blocked"

You receive an email from sbi-alert@sbi-update.xyz (not from sbi.co.in) saying your account will be blocked unless you verify your details at a link. The link leads to a fake website that steals your net banking credentials and OTP.

Fake Profiles

Fake social media profiles are created to deceive, harass, impersonate, or scam women. They are often used in romance scams, blackmail, or to collect personal information for malicious purposes.

Warning Signs
  • Account created very recently with few posts
  • Stock photos used as profile picture
  • Rushing to build emotional intimacy quickly
  • Claims to be in the military, doctor abroad
  • Asks to move conversation to WhatsApp/Telegram
  • Eventually asks for money or gift cards
How to Stay Safe
  • Reverse-search profile photos using Google Images
  • Verify identity through video call before trusting
  • Never send money to someone you've only met online
  • Keep your own profile private and limit personal info
  • Report and block suspicious accounts immediately
  • Inform friends/family if you receive suspicious requests

The "Army Officer" Romance Scam

A woman receives a friend request from an attractive man claiming to be a military officer deployed abroad. After weeks of emotional conversations, he claims to need money for "emergency medical treatment" or to "fly home." The photos are stolen from a real person's account.

Did you know? The IT Act Section 66C (Identity Theft) and Section 66D (Cheating by Impersonation) make creating fake profiles with someone else's identity a punishable offence with up to 3 years imprisonment.

Online Harassment

Online harassment includes cyberstalking, trolling, doxxing, revenge pornography, and sending threatening or abusive messages. Women face disproportionately higher rates of targeted online abuse.

Types of Online Harassment
  • Cyberstalking: Persistent unwanted contact online
  • Trolling: Deliberate offensive or provocative messages
  • Doxxing: Publicly revealing private information
  • Morphing: Editing intimate photos without consent
  • Revenge Porn: Non-consensual intimate image sharing
  • Impersonation: Creating fake accounts in your name
What To Do
  • Document everything — screenshots with timestamps
  • Do NOT engage with harassers — block immediately
  • Report to the platform (Twitter, Instagram, etc.)
  • File a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930
  • Tell a trusted person — you are not alone
  • Consult a lawyer for IPC Section 67A cases

Legal Provisions

IT Act Section 66A — Sending offensive electronic messages
IT Act Section 67A — Publishing/transmitting sexually explicit content: Up to 5 years & ₹10 lakh fine
IPC Section 354D — Cyberstalking: Up to 3 years imprisonment

Privacy Protection

Your digital privacy is your right. Learn practical steps to control your online footprint, secure your accounts, and protect your personal data from being misused.

Device Security
  • Use strong biometric/PIN lock on all devices
  • Keep your OS and apps updated
  • Install apps only from official stores
  • Review app permissions regularly (camera, mic, location)
  • Use a VPN on public Wi-Fi networks
Social Media Privacy
  • Set all profiles to Private
  • Don't share real-time location in posts
  • Disable geotagging on photos
  • Limit who can see your contact info
  • Regularly review connected apps and revoke unused access
Account Security
  • Enable 2FA on email, banking, and social apps
  • Use a password manager (Bitwarden, 1Password)
  • Never reuse passwords across sites
  • Check haveibeenpwned.com for leaked accounts
Email & Communication
  • Use end-to-end encrypted messaging (Signal, WhatsApp)
  • Use ProtonMail for sensitive communications
  • Be cautious sharing personal email publicly
  • Don't open email attachments from unknown senders
